Overview

1. Mechanical Engineering is a field of engineering that deals with the design, construction, and use of machines. 2. It involves the application of engineering principles and techniques to develop innovative solutions.

Subfields

1. Thermodynamics: Studies heat transfer, energy conversion, and fluid dynamics.
2. Mechanics of Materials: Examines stress, strain, and material properties.
3. Dynamics and Vibration: Analyzes motion, vibration, and control systems.
4. Fluid Mechanics: Deals with fluid flow, pressure, and fluid dynamics.
5. Mechatronics: Combines mechanical, electrical, and software engineering.
6. Robotics: Focuses on robot design, control, and automaation.
7. Aerospace Engineering: Involves aircraft, spacecraft, and missile design. 8. Automotive Engineering: Concerned with vehicle design, development, and manufacturing.
9. Biomechanical Engineering: Applies mechanical principles to medical devices and biological systems.
10. Energy Engineering: Develops sustainable energy solutions.
